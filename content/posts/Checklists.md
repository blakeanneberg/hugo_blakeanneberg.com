For video game development, it feels like your building plane while flying. Like flying a plane, checklists are key. Some of the goals that I want to check for any of the games I might make:
- Is Game loop fun? Have a educational/historical component? Highlight advocacy for sustainability (like trains, or environment or construction) 
- Play the video game twice a week, write down bugs, features, thoughts or hopes.
- Create Youtube video or social media post or reach out to game reviewers. 

Some other things that don't fit in a checklist but are to-do lists:
- Merch Store for CO Rail Game
- Plan Changes for V2 for CO Rail Game and figure out development costs for that. 
- Vision/mock-up/GameLoop board for River Game and Coorie Home Game to then make roadmap for game development
- Plan a date to travel along the Front Range and visit the cities and locations of the CRG.

So lots to do! Glad lists are a thing.
