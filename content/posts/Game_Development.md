With the approach of the release of CRG... and I think its a good time to document the journey so far.

Some initial thoughts:

- Its expensive. Having a team help has speed up the project, but its also alot of time and money and resources. 
- Its good to understand the game engine. I have been learning on Boot.Dev alot about back end development. I am also learning Godot from GameDev.tv. These two paid online learning platforms have really helped me better understand how to create new games and have given me ideas on how to make my own game better. This takes alot of time and making it a priority is a must.
- Its a roller coaster. Its very exciting to have the idea, then tell people the idea, but then kinda scary to get feedback, spend time and money on the project, and then think of risks. Keeping a positive outlook and understanding that there is a wider community out there that will enjoy the game is helpful. 

Those are just some thoughts, now need to find reference images and more sounds for the game.
